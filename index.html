Our method is capable of deforming various input meshes towards a variety of text-specified styles. The style can be manifested as fine geometric details, like in the ornate art deco column, or as low-frequency deformations, such as the joints of the cybernetic glove. Our method retains the structural features of the input shape, such as a flat arm on the antique sofa. Moreover, the resultant stylizations are in accordance with prompt semantics and part-aware semantics: the folds in the tropical chair are on the seat and backrest as opposed to the legs, the head of the penguin becomes like the top of a fire hydrant, and the racer bunny's thigh turns into the shape of a wheel.
